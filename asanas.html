Explore Yoga Asanas

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

History: Tadasana is one of the foundational poses in yoga, often used to teach proper alignment and posture.

Benefits: Improves posture, strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles, and helps to relieve back pain.

How To Do: Stand with feet together, arms at sides. Engage thighs, lift chest, and stretch arms overhead. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Vrikshasana (Tree Pose)

History: Vrikshasana is a balancing pose that mimics the posture of a tree, promoting stability and focus.

Benefits: Improves balance, strengthens legs, and opens the hips.

How To Do: Stand on one leg, place the other foot on the inner thigh or calf. Bring hands to prayer position or overhead. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Trikonasana (Triangle Pose)

History: Trikonasana is an essential pose in yoga that stretches the legs and torso, enhancing overall flexibility.

Benefits: Stretches legs, hips, and spine, strengthens the legs, and improves digestion.

How To Do: Stand with legs wide apart, turn one foot out, and reach the same-side arm down to touch the ankle or floor. Extend the other arm up. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

History: Adho Mukha Svanasana is a classic inversion pose that resembles a dog stretching forward.

Benefits: Stretches the back, hamstrings, and calves, strengthens the arms and legs, and improves blood flow.

How To Do: Start on all fours, lift your hips up and back, straighten your legs, and press your heels towards the floor. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

History: Bhujangasana is a backbend pose that mimics the cobra's posture, enhancing spinal flexibility.

Benefits: Strengthens the spine, stretches the chest and shoulders, and helps relieve stress.

How To Do: Lie face down, place your hands under your shoulders, and lift your chest while keeping your lower body on the floor. Hold for 15-30 seconds.

Dhanurasana (Bow Pose)

History: Dhanurasana resembles a bow and arrow, stretching the entire body and enhancing flexibility.

Benefits: Stretches the front body, strengthens the back, and improves posture.

How To Do: Lie on your stomach, bend your knees, hold your ankles, and lift your chest and legs. Hold for 15-30 seconds.

Setu Bandhasana (Bridge Pose)

History: Setu Bandhasana helps to open the chest and strengthen the back, resembling a bridge.

Benefits: Strengthens the back, stretches the chest and spine, and stimulates abdominal organs.

How To Do: Lie on your back, bend your knees, and lift your hips towards the ceiling. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Salamba Sirsasana (Supported Headstand)

History: Salamba Sirsasana is a challenging inversion pose that requires balance and strength.

Benefits: Improves circulation, strengthens the shoulders and arms, and enhances mental focus.

How To Do: Support your head on the floor, interlace your fingers behind your head, lift your legs up, and balance. Hold for 15-30 seconds.

Sarvangasana (Shoulder Stand)

History: Sarvangasana is a classic pose often referred to as the "queen of asanas" due to its numerous benefits.

Benefits: Strengthens the shoulders, improves circulation, and helps to alleviate stress.

How To Do: Lie on your back, lift your legs and hips, and support your lower back with your hands. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Halasana (Plow Pose)

History: Halasana is a pose that stretches the spine and shoulders while resembling a plow.

Benefits: Stretches the shoulders and spine, calms the nervous system, and aids digestion.

How To Do: Lie on your back, lift your legs over your head, and touch the floor behind you. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)

History: Paschimottanasana stretches the entire back and is a classic seated forward fold pose.

Benefits: Stretches the spine and hamstrings, improves digestion, and calms the mind.

How To Do: Sit with legs extended, reach forward to touch your feet, and keep your back straight. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend)

History: Uttanasana is a forward bending pose that releases tension in the back and legs.

Benefits: Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine, and helps relieve stress.

How To Do:Stand with feet hip-width apart, bend forward from the hips, and try to touch the floor. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Ardha Matsyendrasana (Half Lord of the Fishes Pose)

History: This pose is named after the sage Matsyendra and helps in detoxifying the body.

Benefits: Increases spinal flexibility, stimulates abdominal organs, and improves digestion.

How To Do: Sit with one leg bent over the other, twist your torso towards the bent knee, and hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

History: Navasana strengthens the core and helps in improving balance and concentration.

Benefits: Strengthens the abdominal muscles, improves balance, and stimulates the digestive system.

How To Do: Sit with your legs bent, lift them off the floor, extend your arms forward, and hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Virabhadrasana I (Warrior I Pose)

History: This pose is named after the warrior Virabhadra, enhancing strength and focus.

Benefits: Strengthens legs and core, stretches the chest and lungs, and improves concentration.

How To Do: Step one foot back, bend the front knee, and reach your arms overhead. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Virabhadrasana II (Warrior II Pose)

History: Virabhadrasana II is a powerful pose that builds stamina and endurance.

Benefits: Strengthens legs, opens hips and chest, and improves balance.

How To Do: Step one foot back, bend the front knee, and extend arms to the sides, gazing forward. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Virabhadrasana III (Warrior III Pose)

History: This pose enhances balance and strength, named after the warrior Virabhadra.

Benefits: Strengthens the legs, core, and back, and improves balance.

How To Do: Stand on one leg, extend the other leg and arms forward, and keep your torso parallel to the floor. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Garudasana (Eagle Pose)

History: Garudasana is a balancing pose that imitates the posture of an eagle.

Benefits: Improves balance, stretches the shoulders and thighs, and enhances focus.

How To Do: Stand on one leg, wrap the other leg around it, and extend the arms in front, crossing them. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

History: Bakasana is an arm-balancing pose that requires strength and balance.

History: Shavasana is a relaxation pose often performed at the end of a yoga session.
